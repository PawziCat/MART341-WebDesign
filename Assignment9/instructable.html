LEAVE IT!

• How to Train A Puppy •




• Pro Tip: Don’t give them the treat you want them to leave, always treat from the pouch or from a raised surface. If you give them the "leave it" treat, it will actually create confusion about what you really want. If you do not clearly demonstrate or express what behavior you want, you are setting the pup up for failure.

• PLEASE NOTE: When training your puppy keep in mid their breed and genetics. Certian breeds such as scent hounds may struggle with this command around food and wild game. In contrast a sight hound may find leaving small animals or moving objects alone. However, with patience, time and reinforcement any dog can be trained.


• DISCLAIMER: If your pup seems to be struggling, keep returning to foundations. However, these training instructions may not work for every dog and you may need to use other training methods or seek additional help from our trainers.


Materials

  • Treats
  • A Quiet Environment
  • Treat pouch / baggie (optional)
  • Clicker (optional)

Instructions

Step 1

Charge you Praise Word

Start the session by charging your praise word / clicker. Have your puppy perform basic commands like sit, laydown and touch for a couple minutes. Use your praise word / clicker and then treat them.

Step 2

Covering Your Treat

Place treat on the ground and either cover it with your hand, for mouthier pups use your foot. Smaller breeds may learn this better with you crouching.

Step 3

When to Praise

At first do not say anything and wait for your puppy to stop going after the treat, praise them with an energetic yes / good or the clicker and give them a different treat from your pouch. Place the treat near the covered treat.

Step 4

Gaining Focus

Repeat till your puppy no longer is interested in the covered treat and begins to look at you for another treat.

Step 5

Taking It Up A Notch

Start dropping the treat in front of you and covering it quickly. Once the pup looks at you every say yes / good pr use the clicker and feed the treat from your hand this time. Repeat process.

Step 6

Active Choice

Now dropping the treat without covering it unless your puppy starts to go after it. When they look to you praise then treat.

Step 7

Add the Command

Add the command "Leave it," the command should be said firmly a single time. Drop the treat and say "leave it" when the dog looks up at you immediately praise then treat. Repeat process.

Step 8

Impulse Control

Start throwing the treat at the puppy's feet now and use the command leave it. If they leave it, treat them with 2-3 treats one after the other.

Step 9

Reinforce the Command

Finally repeat the process except drop multiple treats. Praise and treat from your hand, give multiple treats one at a time. Either drop more treats or pick them up and drop them again.

• Tips •

  • End on a high note. Always end every session in a positive manner. If your pup shows signs of becoming frustrated, disinterested or tired, swap commands to basic ones for a minute or so and end the session with them still having fun and feeling confident.

  • Work towards reinforcing a bomb proof leave it. Take training out of the home and into different places like the backyard, on walks, at parks, in pet friendly stores and local Farmers Markets. The more places the better! Some places are calm and quiet, making it easy to focus, other places are distraction heavy like a pet store where focus is much harder to maintain. Both have their place in a well rounded training regimen and help create a more stable temperment and establish predictable behavior.

  • Leave it can be used for more than treats or dropped bits of food. It can be diversified to include other animals' food, toys, other dogs, small animals, doorbells, knocks, ect.

  • The command is a foundation in working on all kinds of reactivity from dogs, cars and smaller animals to door bells, barking and sirens. Reactivity is the leading cause of rehoming pets, by training early in puppyhood you can nip potential reactivity issues in the butt before they become life long problems.
